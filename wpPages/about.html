What is Barb’s Book Buddies?

Barb’s Book Buddies is a multifaceted approach to represent independent authors and small publishing houses, to assist writers of all ages to hone their craft, and to explore the global connectedness and authenticity that quality  children’s literature should encompass.  Barb’s Book Buddies primary goal is to instill a love of books and reading at an early age.

Who is Barb?

Great question!  So many answers!  I love my family, teaching (but, honestly my students  are my teachers), reading, solving problems (a.k.a fixing things), laughing (mostly at myself), traveling/camping and the simple things in life i.e. seeing fireflies on summer nights, the smell of a campfire, the taste of a perfectly ripe fruit/vegetable, the feel of moss under my toes, and hearing the cacophony of frogs. I believe the more connections we “allow” ourselves to have in life, the more opportunities we create!  I am an educator of over thirty years. I began to see devices becoming more common than books in children’s hands.  I realized how many amazing stories were “out there” written by people of all ages that truly needed to be told.  I saw an opportunity to connect authors, children and books through the use of technology.  I believe, we are not going to win a fight against technology, nor should we try, we should engage in it, we should embrace it, and we need to have positive alternatives for parents, caregivers and children in the digital world!

I was raised on the simplicity of “Romper Room”, a popular children’s television program, which included stories, songs, rhymes and simple behavioral lessons guided by the hostess.  I was inspired to write a letter to the hostess, and guess what?

I received a response (and I kept that letter for 52 years).  I truly believe that was the tiny seed that was planted that began the inspiration for Barb’s Book Buddies!

Who is Herman?

Herman is my favorite  “STUFFIE”, and he is featured in  every episode of Barb’s Book Buddies.  When I was five years old, my grandpa was traveling to Germany and asked me what I wanted as a souvenir?  My response…a “Teddy Bear”!  The story goes that my grandpa traveled throughout Germany looking for the perfect bear.  He ultimately found “my bear” and kept him on his lap, on the flight home.  As I ran to meet my grandpa, in the airport, he held the bear out to me.  I  immediately named him Herman (which rhymes with German).  Herman went EVERYWHERE with me as a young child, camping, the grocery store, to play in the barn, even the sandbox, which was not such a good idea!
